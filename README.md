# Hermès Claw

> A satirical card game about wanting something you're not supposed to want too much.

---

## What Is This

Hermès Claw is a 10-turn card game about the social mechanics of luxury retail — specifically the unofficial relationship game required to acquire a Hermès bag.

The NPC remembers you. The rules are hidden. There is a pattern; you'll feel it before you understand it.

It's satirical, but sincere. If you've ever stood in a boutique wondering what you did wrong, this game is for you.

---

## How to Play

Each turn you're dealt three cards. Pick one.

Over 10 turns, manage your relationship with the Associate well enough that she decides you deserve a bag.

**The cards:**

| Card | What it is |
|------|------------|
| Boutique Visit | Spend money. Buy something. Or don't. |
| The Associate | Talk to her. How you talk matters. Discuss an outfit. Ask about a bag. |
| Do Nothing | Wait. Patience is its own currency. |
| A Quiet Opportunity | Something rare became available. Your call. |
| Secondary Market | A discreet offer. Think carefully. |
| Cold Shoulder | The temperature shifted. You already know why. |

Not all cards appear every turn. The deck responds to how the game is going.

---

## What You're Working Toward

There are three chapters, each with a target bag. Win a chapter and the next one unlocks — with a different Associate, a different atmosphere, and a harder version of the same desire.

The bags, in order of difficulty:

- **Constance** — the beginning of something
- **Kelly** — structure, restraint, everything
- **Birkin** — there are no words

Each chapter also has a smaller, rarer variant. It won't appear just because you played well. But it might.

---

## Accessories

Buying smaller items along the way — a Twilly, a Carré H scarf, shoes, a bracelet — isn't just spending. Each piece quietly changes the dynamic. How, exactly, is something you discover.

The everyday bags (Evelyne, Picotin, Garden Party, and others) are offered when the moment is right. They're not the goal. But they're not nothing, either.

---

## Tips (Vague, Intentionally)

- The way you talk to her is not neutral.
- Asking too soon is noticed. So is never asking.
- Money helps. Money is not the point.
- Some patterns matter. Not all patterns matter.
- She remembers everything. Including what you did last time.

---

## The NPC Remembers You

Your history is saved in your browser. Return visits feel different. The Associate's opening line is not random.

To start fresh: open your browser's developer tools → Application → Local Storage → delete `hermes_claw_profile`.
